Professional Profile

I am a PhD candidate in applied statistics, with a solid background in spatial data analysis, econometric modeling, and narrative visualization. My academic trajectory combines research, teaching, and content development, with particular emphasis on entropy and complexity metrics, spatial systems, educational innovation, and data-informed communication.

My work explores phenomena ranging from three-dimensional seismic structures to educational inequality and social mobility, while also developing teaching strategies based on digital content, interactive visualizations, project-based learning, and visual storytelling. I believe software should not be taught merely as an operational tool, but as a means to think, model, and communicate with data in an ethical, effective, and transformative way.

Teaching and Academic Development

I design and teach courses in econometrics, spatial data analysis, time series in R, data visualization and storytelling, and Power BI for academic and professional audiences. My teaching practice is strongly oriented toward applied learning, reproducibility, and the integration of computational thinking into disciplinary training.

I have also developed educational interventions based on short-form videos, digital platforms, interactive dashboards, and structured assessment frameworks, particularly in statistics and econometrics education.
